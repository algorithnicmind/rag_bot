In 2026, Nexus Corporation launched its revolutionary quantum processor, the "NexusQ-1". This processor was capable of performing calculations 100 times faster than its predecessor, thanks to its 500-qubit architecture. 

During the initial testing phase, the NexusQ-1 reduced data processing times for weather simulations from 3 weeks to just 4 hours. The cost of development was approximately $4.2 billion over a span of five years.

The lead scientist, Dr. Aris Thorne, noted that the biggest challenge was maintaining cryogenic temperatures below 15 millikelvins to prevent qubit decoherence. The NexusQ-1 is expected to hit the enterprise market in Q3 2027, starting at a base price of $25 million per unit.
